Platform reliability engineer interviews assess your ability to ensure the availability, performance, and resilience of internal developer platforms and shared infrastructure services. Interviewers evaluate your expertise in SRE practices applied to platform services, SLO design, incident management, capacity planning, and your ability to maintain the reliability of systems that hundreds of engineering teams depend on daily.

Example Platform Reliability Engineer Interview Questions

  • How would you define SLOs for an internal platform serving 500 engineers across 100 services?
  • Describe your approach to implementing error budgets and using them to balance reliability with feature velocity.
  • How do you design a monitoring and alerting strategy that catches platform issues before users report them?
  • Design a capacity planning system for a platform that handles variable workloads.
  • How would you conduct a chaos engineering experiment on a critical platform service?
  • Describe your incident management process for a platform outage affecting all downstream services.
  • How do you implement graceful degradation in platform services during partial failures?
  • Design an automated canary analysis system for platform service deployments.
  • How would you reduce mean time to recovery for platform incidents?
  • Describe your approach to toil reduction and automation for platform operations.
  • How do you communicate platform reliability status and incidents to consuming teams?
  • Design a post-incident review process that drives systematic reliability improvements.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is this different from SRE?

Platform reliability engineers focus specifically on the reliability of internal platforms and developer infrastructure. SRE is broader and may cover any production service. The principles are the same but the scope is platform-specific.

What SRE practices should I know?

SLOs, error budgets, toil budgets, incident management, blameless post-mortems, and chaos engineering. Read the Google SRE book and understand how these practices apply to platform services specifically.

How important is coding?

Important. Platform reliability engineers build automation, monitoring tools, and reliability infrastructure. Go and Python are the most common languages. Understanding Prometheus, Grafana, and observability pipelines is expected.

What scale should I prepare to discuss?

Be ready to discuss platforms handling thousands of deployments per day, millions of API calls, and serving hundreds of engineering teams. Understanding how reliability challenges change at scale is essential.

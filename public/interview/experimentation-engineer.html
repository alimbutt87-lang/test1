Experimentation engineer interviews assess your ability to build and operate platforms that enable rigorous A/B testing and controlled experiments at scale. Interviewers evaluate your expertise in experimental design, statistical analysis, randomization systems, metric computation, and your ability to build experimentation infrastructure that enables product teams to make data-driven decisions with confidence and speed.

Example Experimentation Engineer Interview Questions

  • How would you design an experimentation platform that supports 500 concurrent experiments?
  • Describe your approach to implementing a randomization system that ensures unbiased assignment.
  • How do you calculate the required sample size and experiment duration for a given test?
  • Design a metric computation pipeline that detects statistically significant results in real time.
  • How would you handle interaction effects between multiple overlapping experiments?
  • Describe your experience with sequential testing and when it is preferable to fixed-horizon tests.
  • How do you implement guardrail metrics that automatically halt harmful experiments?
  • Design a system for experiment analysis that handles non-normal distributions and outliers.
  • How would you build an experimentation platform that supports both A/B tests and multi-armed bandits?
  • Describe your approach to network effects and switchback experiments in marketplace settings.
  • How do you communicate experiment results and limitations to product stakeholders?
  • Design an experiment review process that ensures statistical rigor before results are acted upon.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much statistics do I need to know?

Strong applied statistics is required. Understand hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, power analysis, multiple testing corrections, and Bayesian methods. You should be able to design experiments that produce valid, actionable results.

What programming languages are expected?

Python for statistical analysis and platform development, SQL for metric computation, and often Go or Java for the randomization service. R is sometimes used for statistical modeling.

Which companies hire for this role?

Tech companies with large user bases — Meta, Netflix, Airbnb, Uber, LinkedIn, Microsoft, and Spotify. Any company running experiments at scale needs this expertise. Optimizely and similar platforms also hire.

How do I prepare for design questions?

Study the experimentation platforms described in engineering blogs from Netflix, Airbnb, and Microsoft. Understand the architecture of randomization services, metric pipelines, and analysis frameworks.

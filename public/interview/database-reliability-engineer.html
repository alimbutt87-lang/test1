Database reliability engineer interviews assess your ability to ensure high availability, performance, and disaster recovery for database systems at scale. Interviewers evaluate your expertise in database internals, replication architectures, automated failover, capacity planning, and applying site reliability engineering principles to data layer operations.

Example Database Reliability Engineer Interview Questions

  • How do you design a database architecture that achieves 99.99% uptime?
  • Describe your approach to automated failover for a primary-replica database cluster.
  • How do you handle database schema migrations with zero downtime?
  • Explain the trade-offs between synchronous and asynchronous replication.
  • How do you perform capacity planning for a rapidly growing database workload?
  • Describe your experience managing databases on Kubernetes using operators.
  • How do you detect and resolve database performance degradation before it impacts users?
  • What is your strategy for database backup, recovery testing, and disaster recovery?

Frequently Asked Questions

Which databases should I know?

PostgreSQL and MySQL are the most commonly tested relational databases. Familiarity with at least one NoSQL system like MongoDB, Cassandra, or DynamoDB is expected. Cloud-managed services like RDS, Cloud SQL, and Aurora are also important.

Is this different from a traditional DBA?

Yes. DBREs apply SRE principles—automation, observability, SLOs—to database operations. The role emphasizes coding, infrastructure-as-code, and self-healing systems over manual administration.

How important is programming?

Essential. You should be proficient in Python or Go for automation, Terraform for infrastructure provisioning, and SQL for query analysis. Writing custom monitoring and tooling is a core part of the role.

What scale should I be prepared to discuss?

Be ready to discuss fleets of hundreds of database instances, petabyte-scale data, millions of queries per second, and multi-region replication. Interviewers want to see you think about operational complexity at scale.

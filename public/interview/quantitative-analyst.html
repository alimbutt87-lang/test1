Frequently Asked Questions

What math background is required?

Strong foundations in probability, statistics, stochastic calculus, and linear algebra are essential. Be prepared for brain teasers testing quantitative reasoning, derivations of financial formulas, and statistical problem-solving. Most quant roles expect graduate-level mathematical sophistication. Practice probability puzzles and mathematical finance concepts regularly.

Which programming languages are important?

Python is dominant for research and analysis, with strong libraries for quantitative work. C++ matters for low-latency trading systems. SQL is essential for data access. Know pandas, numpy, and scipy well. Some firms use specialized languages like R or Matlab. Focus on writing efficient, correct code for numerical computations.

Do I need finance experience?

Financial knowledge helps but varies by role. Trading quants need market intuition, while risk quants need regulatory understanding. Research quants focus more on statistical modeling. Be prepared to discuss financial concepts relevant to the role—derivatives, portfolio theory, or risk measures—even if your background is primarily mathematical or computational.

How hard are quant interviews?

Quant interviews are known for being challenging, with multiple rounds testing different skills. Expect probability brainteasers, mathematical derivations, coding exercises, and behavioral questions. Preparation is essential—practice mental math, work through books like 'Heard on the Street,' and review fundamental concepts thoroughly before interviewing.
